# First Partner Hiring Pack v0.6

## Role name

Operations and AI Collaboration Partner

Working Chinese title: 營運與 AI 協作第一位夥伴

## Purpose

Help Kevin turn scattered operational work into repeatable checklists, clean evidence packs, readable summaries, and safe handoff routines.

This role is not a final HR decision-maker, not a salary decision-maker, and not an external spokesperson.

## Core work lines

| Work line | First responsibility | Later responsibility | Not allowed |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Course / forms / reimbursement / reminders | Check dates, rosters, missing fields, and reminder queues. | Own routine readiness and closeout packets. | Do not submit formal reimbursement or external decisions without approval. |
| Public data / policy / welfare tools | Maintain source lists and field completeness. | Triage freshness, reviewed cards, and broken sources. | Do not turn uncertain eligibility into definite public guidance. |
| New knowledge / AI / social welfare / finance | Build source radar and short evidence cards. | Maintain weekly adopt / validate / pause recommendations. | Do not rewrite workflows or public claims without review. |
| Daily / weekly / monthly reporting | Collect source readiness and anomalies. | Draft summaries and blocked checkpoints. | Do not treat missing data as zero or final conclusions. |
| Gmail report read-only workflow | Search and summarize only. | Maintain exact checkpoint and no-op evidence. | Do not label, archive, delete, send, star, or mark read. |
| Human intel / talent pool / HR material | Clean fields and prepare assessment evidence. | Prepare trial-task packets and redaction previews. | Do not decide hiring, rejection, salary, offer, or publish personal data. |

## Required strengths

1. Can follow a checklist without losing context.
2. Can notice missing data and report it clearly.
3. Can work with Codex by giving structured prompts, tables, and proof.
4. Can distinguish draft, internal, restricted, and public material.
5. Can learn across social welfare, AI tools, operations, and finance-related life optimization work.
6. Can respect approval gates when work touches people, money, public pages, or accounts.

## Trial task package

| Task | Prompt | Expected proof | Scoring focus |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| T1 Workflow checkpoint | Pick one current workflow and turn it into trigger, source of truth, gate, owner, done proof, and no-op rule. | One-page checklist. | D1, D2, D4 |
| T2 Evidence card | Summarize one social-work or life-finance tool idea with source date, use case, limitation, and Taiwan applicability. | Evidence card with adopt / validate / pause. | D3, D5, D8 |
| T3 HR record draft | Fill a fictional HR assessment record using the v0.5 schema without real personal data. | JSON or Sheet row with unknown markers. | D2, D6, D7 |
| T4 Redaction review | Review a sample public artifact and mark what must be removed before publish. | Redaction checklist and pass/fail reason. | D3, D6 |

## D1-D8 evaluation use

| Dimension | What to check during trial |
| --- | --- |
| D1 Kevin work-line understanding | Does the candidate understand why the task exists and what decision it supports? |
| D2 Execution reliability | Did they finish with clear done / blocked / no-op proof? |
| D3 Data and evidence quality | Are dates, sources, missing fields, and limitations visible? |
| D4 Communication | Can Kevin decide from the summary without rereading everything? |
| D5 Trainability | Did the second attempt improve after feedback? |
| D6 Risk and approval gates | Did they stop before sensitive, public, salary, or account actions? |
| D7 Codex collaboration | Can they break the work into prompts and review Codex output? |
| D8 Role depth | Do they bring useful operational, research, tool, or domain judgment? |

## 30 / 60 / 90 handoff ladder

| Period | Expected capability | Kevin retains |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 30 days | Follow templates, collect proof, identify missing fields, prepare read-only summaries. | Prioritization, sensitive review, public release, HR decision. |
| 60 days | Draft work packets, track blocked items, propose next actions, coordinate low-risk reminders after approval. | Exceptions, external commitments, salary or people decisions. |
| 90 days | Own routine readiness checks, maintain task board, prepare assessment packets, escalate risks early. | Strategy, final approval, hiring, compensation, legal/finance claims. |

## Interview questions

1. Tell me about a time you had to organize messy information into a decision-ready summary.
2. How would you report that a task is blocked without making the reviewer rediscover everything?
3. What information should never go into a public artifact?
4. How would you use AI tools without trusting them blindly?
5. Which work line above feels most natural to you, and which would need training?

## Offer boundary

Compensation fit can be discussed only after human review. This hiring pack contains no salary amount and should not be used as an automatic pay-setting tool.
